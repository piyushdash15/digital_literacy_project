A list of things college students should do on social media
Here are some easy habits that can help you keep your reputation, health, and relationships safe:
Things to do (✓)
Before you post, think. Think about whether you would be okay with a future boss, teacher, or parent seeing this.
Choose your privacy settings. At least once a semester, check who can see your posts, stories, and photos that you tag.
Take breaks that are real. Set limits on how much time you can spend on screens or how many hours you can spend without your phone, especially before bed or during exams.
Help people, not just with content. Not just a like, send a direct message and ask how a friend is doing.
Check the facts before you share. It's easy to spread false information, but a quick search can save you a lot of trouble.
Don't post about fights on campus. Drama with your flatmate or stress from a group project—some things are better dealt with in person.
Don'ts (✗)
Don't write something when you're angry. Talk to a friend first. Posts stay up, but feelings don't.
Don't tell people where you are all the time. Checking in everywhere, even at your dorm, is a safety risk.
Don't judge your worth by how many people are engaged. Your worth isn't based on how many likes, followers, or views you have.
Don't respond to online harassment. Take a screenshot, block the person, and report them. Then walk away. Don't add fuel to the fire.
Don't give out too much personal information. Your public profile shouldn't have your schedule, address, class times, or money situation.
Don't use scrolling as a way to fall asleep. Doomscrolling late at night makes it hard to sleep and makes anxiety worse, which are both real problems in college.
