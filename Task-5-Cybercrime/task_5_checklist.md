The General Online Safety tips are listed below:

Use a strong password that is different for each of your accounts

Consider creating a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols to create a strong password. Do not use the same password for your college email or UPI applications or social networking accounts. Use a password manager like Bitwarden to manage your passwords (this is free).



Turn on 2FA on accounts where it is available.

If someone steals your password, the only way into your account will be through an OTP or an authenticator code. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on any account that offers this feature.



Never click on an unknown link in a WhatsApp message or an SMS.

Phishing links usually arrive with messages like "You have won a prize!" or "Your package has been held." If a link looks suspicious, do not click on it — even if the link comes from someone you know.



Do not log into accounts using public computers or unsecured Wi-Fi

Both public cafe Wi-Fi and computers in computer labs at school are subject to monitoring. If you must use public wifi, avoid using any banking applications or entering any passwords through the public wifi. If it is possible for you to use your mobile data for sensitive transactions, please do so.



Lock your phone at all times using either a PIN or a biometric lock

If a phone is lost or stolen while it is unlocked, the person who finds it will have complete access to your college UPI applications, email, and social networking accounts. Set your phone to automatically lock after 30 seconds of inactivity.

Use caution when sharing personal information online (ex.); Do not share items such as phone numbers, addresses, schedules etc. to your public profile. Scammer’s use information from social media to develop targeted scams against individuals.





UPI Financial Safety (Tips from UPI)

Tip #1 Only you can use your UPI PIN to send money through UPI. Simply hang up if someone asks you for your UPI PIN so they can "confirm" a payment you're receiving.

Tip #2 Always properly review the request before approving it. A UPI collect request is a request from someone else to collect money from your UPI account. Review the details of the transaction on the UPI app and validate the UPI ID of the person requesting your money. If you're still unsure, say no and then call them.

Never provide passwords, PINs or OTPs to anyone over the phone or in a confidential message.

Your OTP will never be requested from your bank, UPI app, or any government agency. If someone from a "bank" calls you and asks for your OTP, this is FRAUD. Block the number and end the call.

* National Helpline(1930):- Calls 24/7 to freeze transactions and report fraud.
* Online Portal(cybercrime.gov.in):- File a complaint of financial fraud or cyberbullying.
